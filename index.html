Civil Engineering Personal Catalog

Jude Ashoff, Kaleb Raymond, and Shane Gilbert

Conjecture

Course scheduling is a process each student must undergo numerous times at the University of Pittsburgh. The university website provides sufficient information for the process; however, it is scattered throughout several pages and difficult to navigate. Lists of valid electives change, course requirements may update, and degree requirements are difficult to keep track of. We proposed a tool that condenses all of the information into a clear and concise page. In this case, we created a virtual course catalog specific to the civil engineering degree. The goal of the page is to reduce confusion and time spent on scheduling for each semester.

Definition

Our proposal will allow students who are pursuing a Civil Engineering major to view courses in their field, create a checklist of classes that theyâ€™ve taken, keep track of their unfulfilled major requirements, and view sample schedules.

Quality

One of the most confusing things to do each semester is scheduling classes on the MyPitt website. This is important because it will save students from the aggravation of finding the right classes and not knowing what courses to enroll in. Students AND Advisors will gain from this - make it easier for both. If we do not do anything, the same, old, confusing scheduler will still be running.

Policy

Moving forward, several changes would enhance this course planner in the future. Given the short timeframe to work on the project, some features did not make it into the final version. Time consuming tasks such as writing descriptions for elective classes, including professors of each course, and listing out class meeting times were all cut from the final product. If the university wanted to make the planner more complete, they would implement these features into our code, and provide students with an even more beneficial tool. Furthermore, the code may be adapted to any other major offered at the university. By iterating through the work completed here, a more user-friendly version of student center can be created, which would provide the same help to students with any other degree.
